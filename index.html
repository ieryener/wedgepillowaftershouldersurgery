wedge pillow after shoulder surgery

Shoulder surgery can be a difficult (and painful!) ordeal, and recovery is often slow and uncomfortable. Thankfully, there are several products available to help make the process more bearable. One such product is the wedge pillow! Though these pillows may seem like an insignificant item, they can really make a difference in shoulder pain relief after surgery.

Unlike many other pillows, wedge pillows are designed specifically for those who have undergone shoulder surgery; they provide support and keep the arm elevated while sleeping or resting. This helps reduce pressure on the shoulder which can lessen pain and swelling. Another great benefit of using a wedge pillow is that it encourages proper posture during sleep. Instead of slumping over in bed, which can cause additional strain on the healing shoulder, one will be able to rest with good posture without even realizing it!

Moreover, wedge pillows are relatively inexpensive compared to many other post-surgery aids, making them a great option for those looking for some extra comfort during their recovery period. Despite their low cost though, these pillows still provide quality support that's sure to make life easier after surgery (especially considering all that comes with it!).

All in all, wedge pillows are an effective and affordable way to help ease discomfort following shoulder surgery. They provide excellent support for one's arm while encouraging good posture during sleep - something that's key to any successful recovery! So if you're recovering from shoulder surgery (or know someone who is), consider getting yourself a wedge pillow! It could make all the difference!

History

Though the exact origin is unknown, use of pillows evolved in animals well into prehistory, the earliest examples including reptiles and mammals resting their heads on themselves, and one another, to support the head and neck.[1] Animals, including humans, evolved use of inanimate objects in their nests out of wood and stone as pillows.[1] Since domestication, many animals have also learned to make use of human-made pillows and cushions, as well as to rest on members of their own and other species, for this purpose.[1]

Sometime between 5 and 23 million years ago tree-dwelling great apes began building sleeping platforms, including wooden pillows, to improve their sleep.[6] According to studies on chimpanzees that sleep up to eight to nine hours a night using specifically selected ironwood pillows, sturdy pillows enabled great apes to escape being hunted by night predators and not fall out of the trees while asleep.[6] It is likely that this was necessitated by the evolution of large, energy-consuming brains.[6] Though it may also have led to longer periods of REM sleep, that in turn increased their cognitive capacity.[6]

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a wedge pillow?
A wedge pillow is an angled cushion that provides support for the head, neck, and shoulders while sleeping.
Is a wedge pillow recommended after shoulder surgery?
Yes, a wedge pillow can help provide support and reduce pressure on the shoulder during sleep following shoulder surgery.
How long should I use a wedge pillow after surgery?
It is recommended to use a wedge pillow for at least two weeks following shoulder surgery in order to ensure proper healing and recovery time.
Are there any risk associated with using a wedge pillow after shoulder surgery?
Using a wedge pillow after shoulder surgery can cause discomfort or pain due to increased pressure on the wound site or by not allowing the joint to move freely during rehabilitation exercises. Therefore it is important to talk to your doctor about what type of cushioning might be best for you before using one post-surgery.
What are some tips for using a wedge pillow after shoulder surgery?
Some tips for using a wedge pillow include making sure it fits properly so that your head and neck are supported; adjusting the angle according to comfort level; avoiding sleeping on either side of the affected area; and switching sides regularly throughout the night as much as possible (if comfortable).
